Customer Churn Prediction

End-to-end ML pipeline to identify at-risk customers and enable proactive retention.

← Back to Portfolio

Overview

Customer churn directly impacts recurring revenue. The objective of this project was to build an interpretable machine learning pipeline that predicts which customers are likely to leave, so the business can take action (discounts, targeted outreach, service improvements) before the churn happens.

Challenge

Identify at-risk customers early with reliable signals and explainability for business stakeholders. Key constraints included categorical-heavy data, class imbalance, and the need for actionable insights.

Outcome

Achieved ~0.86 ROC-AUC with XGBoost and high recall on the churn class. Top drivers included Contract Type, Tenure, and MonthlyCharges. Delivered a simple scoring workflow and a dashboard-ready output table for BI consumption.

Dataset

Public telecom churn dataset (7k+ records, 20+ features): demographics, account information, service usage, and billing. Target: Churn (Yes/No). Split: 80/20 train-test.

  • Size: ~7,043 rows · 21 features
  • Feature types: categorical-heavy (contract, internet service), some numeric (tenure, charges)
  • Imbalance: churn rate around 25–30%

Approach

1) Data Preparation

  • Handled missing values and standardized binary fields (Yes/No → 1/0).
  • One-hot encoded multi-class categorical variables (e.g., Contract, InternetService).
  • Scaled numeric columns (tenure, MonthlyCharges, TotalCharges).
  • Train/validation split with stratification to preserve churn ratio.

2) EDA Highlights

  • Month-to-month contracts and short tenure strongly correlated with churn.
  • Higher MonthlyCharges slightly increased churn propensity.
  • Paperless billing and electronic payments showed notable patterns in churn cohorts.

3) Modeling

  • Baselines: Logistic Regression and Random Forest.
  • Best: XGBoost with class-weighting and hyperparameter tuning (grid search on depth, estimators, learning rate).
  • Evaluation metrics: Accuracy, Precision/Recall, F1, ROC-AUC; business emphasis on recall to minimize missed churners.

4) Explainability

  • Feature importance for top drivers (Contract, Tenure, MonthlyCharges, PaymentMethod).
  • Partial dependence / SHAP (optional) for stakeholder understanding.
